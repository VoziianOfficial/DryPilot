What Cookies Are

Cookies are small text files that websites may store on a visitor’s browser or device. Similar technologies may include local storage, session storage, pixels, tags, scripts, or analytics identifiers.

These technologies can help websites remember preferences, measure performance, understand visitor behavior, and maintain basic functionality.

Types of Cookies We May Use

  • Essential cookies or storage used to support basic website functionality.
  • Preference cookies or local storage used to remember cookie banner choices.
  • Analytics cookies used to understand how visitors interact with pages and forms.
  • Performance cookies used to help improve loading, layout, and user experience.
  • Security-related technologies used to help prevent misuse and maintain site integrity.

How Cookies May Be Used

Cookies and similar technologies may be used to support site performance, remember privacy preferences, understand visitor interactions, improve forms, and evaluate page experience.
